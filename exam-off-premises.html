  • Every offense carries the same flat fine no matter the licensee's history
  • Penalties escalate with each offense (fine, then a larger fine, then a fine plus license suspension, then revocation), and a first offense can be reduced to a reprimand if the licensee trained all staff
  • Only the fourth offense has a consequence; the first three are informal warnings

Correct answer: 1

The practical point is the escalation and the training incentive, not a specific dollar figure.
