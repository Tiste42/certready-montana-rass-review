Welcome to Montana Responsible Alcohol Sales and Service Training

s1-c1 | text

Welcome to CertReady's Montana Responsible Alcohol Sales and Service training, the on-premises track. Over the next two hours you will learn the practical knowledge and the Montana-specific law you need to serve, mix, or supervise alcohol service legally and safely under Montana's Responsible Alcohol Sales, Service, and Delivery Act.

Outcomes: K1

s1-c1b | callout

About 'RASS'. You'll see this acronym throughout the course and on Montana's verification database. RASS stands for Responsible Alcohol Sales and Service. As of January 1, 2026, the Act is officially named the Responsible Alcohol Sales, Service, and Delivery Act (HB 211 added 'Delivery' to cover third-party drivers), but the industry still calls the program RASS. When we say 'Montana RASS,' that's what we mean.

Outcomes: K1

s1-c2 | callout

MCA 16-4-1005 (summary): Montana law requires every licensee to ensure that each employee authorized to sell, serve, or deliver alcohol, plus each immediate supervisor, completes a state-approved training program within 60 days of the employee's date of hire and every 3 years thereafter. The statute's operative subsection (1)(a) requires "successful completion of training"; the timing requirements are set out elsewhere in the same section.

Outcomes: K1

s1-c3 | text

Effective January 1, 2026, Montana law requires two distinct training tracks: an on-premises course of at least two hours (this course), and a separate off-premises course of at least one hour. The two tracks share a lot of universal content but the on-premises track goes deeper on intervention, dram shop, and table-side cut-off scenarios because that is where most over-service incidents happen.

Outcomes: K1

s1-c4 | callout

Why this matters now. HB 157 (2025), HB 196 (2025), and HB 211 (2025) changed Montana's alcohol-server framework on January 1, 2026. The on/off split is new. Digital ID coverage is new. And third-party delivery now has its own license and training rules. Even servers who took an older RASS course need refreshed training to be compliant.

s2-c2 | text

After all modules are complete and you have spent at least 120 minutes on content, the final exam will unlock. The exam is 40 multiple-choice questions drawn from a 64-question pool. You need 80% to pass under ARM 42.13.907 (adopted March 7, 2026). If you score 70-79% on your first attempt, you may retake the final exam only. If you score below 70%, you must retake the entire program before another final-exam attempt.

Your certificate and how it gets to your employer

s3-c1 | text

When you pass the final exam, CertReady issues you a certificate immediately. Your CertReady certificate is your Montana training credential. There is no separate state-issued license to chase. Save the PDF, send a copy to your employer, and keep your certificate number handy in case anyone needs to verify it.

Outcomes: K1

s3-c2 | callout

Verification. Anyone (your employer, an auditor, the regulator) can verify your training at svc.mt.gov/dor/liquorserver/public/certsearch.aspx using your last name and date of birth. Per ARM 42.13.904 (as adopted March 7, 2026), CertReady submits training records electronically to Montana CARD within two weeks of your completion via the CARD-approved secure electronic process.

Outcomes: K1

s3-c2b | callout

Did your employer sign you up? If your bar, restaurant, or restaurant group is using CertReady, they have a business dashboard at certready.org/business. They can see your training progress in real time, get a notification when you pass, and see your expiration date so they can remind you when it's time to renew. You don't need to do anything for this. If they enrolled you with their CertReady code, the link is already set up. You'll still get your own copy of the certificate by email.

Outcomes: K1

s3-c3 | text

Your certificate is valid for 3 years from the completion date. About 60 days before it expires, CertReady will email you a reminder so you can re-take and stay compliant.
