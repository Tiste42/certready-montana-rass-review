MT-001

You hire a new bartender who starts March 1. By when must she finish RASS training to keep the licensee compliant under Montana law?

  1. a. Before her first shift on March 1
  2. b. Within 30 days (by March 31)
  3. c. Within 60 days (by April 30) Correct
  4. d. Within 90 days (by May 30)

Correct answer: c. Within 60 days (by April 30)

MCA 16-4-1005: every employee must complete RASS within 60 days of hire.

MT-020

Montana's administrative penalty on a LICENSEE for sale-to-minor violations is a progressive schedule over a rolling 3-year window. What does that structure mean in practice, and what can reduce a first offense?

  1. a. Every offense carries the same flat fine no matter the licensee's history
  2. b. Penalties escalate with each offense (fine, then a larger fine, then a fine plus license suspension, then revocation), and a first offense can be reduced to a reprimand if the licensee trained all staff Correct
  3. c. Only the fourth offense has a consequence; the first three are informal warnings
  4. d. The schedule resets to zero after each individual violation

Correct answer: b. Penalties escalate with each offense (fine, then a larger fine, then a fine plus license suspension, then revocation), and a first offense can be reduced to a reprimand if the licensee trained all staff

ARM 42.13.101 sets a progressive schedule over a rolling 3-year window: each offense is penalized more severely than the last, up to license revocation. A documented staff-training program can reduce a first offense to a reprimand, so ongoing RASS training directly lowers the licensee's regulatory risk. The practical point is the escalation and the training incentive, not a specific dollar figure.

MT-022

Which of these is a permitted provider, under Montana's narrow private-setting carve-out, of an alcoholic beverage (in a nonintoxicating quantity) to a person under 21?

MT-023

Under Montana's narrow private-setting carve-out, provision of alcohol to a person under 21 is limited to a 'nonintoxicating quantity.'

Correct answer: a. True

MCA 16-6-305(1)(a) limits the exception to 'a nonintoxicating quantity,' defined in subsection (1)(c) as a quantity that does not produce a BAC above 0.05 or substantial/visible mental or physical impairment.

MT-027

A customer drives himself sober from your bar and crashes a week later. The crash victim sues your bar under MCA 27-1-710. Does this case meet any of the three dram shop triggers?

  1. a. Yes - driving home alone is a trigger
  2. b. Yes - any later crash creates liability
  3. c. No - none of the three triggers apply (sale-to-minor, service-to-visibly-intoxicated, or forced consumption) Correct
  4. d. Yes - the bar should have asked about driving

Correct answer: c. No - none of the three triggers apply (sale-to-minor, service-to-visibly-intoxicated, or forced consumption)

MCA 27-1-710 requires one of three specific triggers. Sober service that ends in a later, unrelated incident does not create dram shop exposure.

MT-028

A Montana jury awards $400,000 in noneconomic damages in a dram shop case. After the statutory cap, how much can the plaintiff actually collect for noneconomic damages?

  1. a. $50,000
  2. b. $100,000
  3. c. $250,000 Correct
  4. d. Full $400,000 (no cap applies)

Correct answer: c. $250,000

MCA 27-1-710(10): noneconomic damages are capped at $250,000 per dram shop event. Economic damages (medical, lost wages) are not capped.

MT-029

In a Montana dram-shop case, a jury can use a customer's post-service BAC reading as evidence that they were visibly intoxicated AT THE TIME of service.

  1. a. True
  2. b. False Correct

Correct answer: b. False

MCA 27-1-710(6) explicitly bars post-service BAC and post-service conduct from the visibility analysis. Only what was observable at the bar counts.

MT-030

An incident occurs on March 1, 2026. By what date must the injured party serve certified-mail notice on the licensee to preserve a Montana dram-shop claim?

  1. a. Within 30 days (by March 31)
  2. b. Within 60 days (by April 30)
  3. c. Within 90 days (by May 30)
  4. d. Within 180 days (by August 28) Correct

Correct answer: d. Within 180 days (by August 28)

MCA 27-1-710 requires the injured party to serve certified-mail notice WITHIN 180 days of the sale or service. Notice served after day 180 forfeits the dram-shop claim. The civil action itself must commence within 2 years of the sale or service.
